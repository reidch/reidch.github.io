# Submerged Church

This project is a real-time cinematic animation built around a flooded church environment. Through water, lighting, fog, cloth motion and architectural space, I aimed to create a quiet, mysterious and slightly surreal visual experience.

Rather than presenting each graphical effect independently, the project explores how multiple real-time elements can work together to establish a coherent atmosphere. Reflections and refractions reshape the light inside the church, volumetric fog adds spatial depth, and subtle environmental animation gives movement and life to the otherwise static architecture.

## Visual Direction

The scene uses the church structure and flooded interior as its central visual elements. A restrained color palette, directional lighting and volumetric effects establish the composition and depth of the environment.

The visual direction focuses on:

- Monumental architectural scale
- The movement and reflective quality of water
- Light passing through fog and interior space
- A quiet and mysterious atmosphere within an abandoned environment

## Technical Implementation

The animation was created in Unreal Engine and includes:

- Real-time environment construction and materials
- Water and reflection effects
- Volumetric fog and atmospheric lighting
- Cloth and environmental animation
- Camera design and Sequencer
- Real-time rendering and final video production

## Creative Goal

Through this project, I explored how real-time rendering can contribute to visual storytelling. Instead of treating water, fog and materials as isolated demonstrations, I focused on how they could support one another and create a unified emotional experience.
